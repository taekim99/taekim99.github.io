CSE 215: Foundations of Computer Science

Introduction to the logical and mathematical foundations of computer science. Topics include functions, relations, and sets; recursion; elementary logic; and mathematical induction and other proof techniques.

CSE 216: Programming Abstractions

Intermediate-level programming concepts and paradigms, including functional programming, object-orientation, basics of type systems, memory management, program and data abstractions, parameter passing, modularity, and parallel programming.

CSE 220: Systems Fundamentals I

Introduces systems-level programming concepts using the C language and assembly language, and explores the correspondence of programming constructs in these languages.

CSE 303: Introduction to the Theory of Computation

An introduction to the abstract notions encountered in machine computation. Topics include finite automata, regular expressions, and formal languages, with emphasis on regular and context-free grammars.

CSE 312 Social, Legal, and Ethical Issues in Computing

This course deals with the impact of computers on us as individuals and on our society.

CSE 373: Analysis of Algorithms

Mathematical analysis of a variety of computer algorithms including searching, sorting, matrix multiplication, fast Fourier transform, and graph algorithms. Time and space complexity. Upper-bound, lower- bound, and average-case analysis. Introduction to NP completeness.
